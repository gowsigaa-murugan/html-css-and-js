About Nature


Earth Day is celebrated annually on April 22nd and marks a pivotal moment in the modern environmental movement. The day originated in 1970, inspired by the 1969 Santa Barbara oil spill when former Wisconsin senator Gaylord Nelson called for a nationwide environmental teach-in. The first Earth Day brought 20 million Americans together from all walks of life to encourage a sustainable environment. They set the scene for what would later be a major mobilization and environmental awareness of public opinion in the country toward environmental issues. Today, you may ask “What countries celebrate Earth Day?”. Earth Day has become one of the major events on the planet, celebrated as a "global event" by one billion people from hundreds of countries.
